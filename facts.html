Did you know ...

Those who are at most risk for coronavirus are those who have underlying health conditions such as heart problems, asthma, and autoimmune diseases.

The reason why everyone says to wash your hands is that hand soap contains an ingredient that breaks down the outer envelope (made from peptidoglycan) of the virus. 


You can become infected through the spread of droplets, from someone's cough, sneeze, or when they talk. That is why you should stay 6 feet apart. 

COVID actually spreads slower than influenza, but the incubation period is longer, thus it's harder to know who has it and who doesn't.

The amount of people who could have been asymptomatic can be anywhere from 25-50%.

The most important thing you can do is wash your hands and avoid touching your nose and mouth. ​Stay away from people who may be having symptoms. ​Wear a mask when in public and practice social distancing of at least 6 feet.
