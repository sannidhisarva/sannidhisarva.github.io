What Happened:

As people have been starting to go outside more, the cases have escalated rapidly in some states in the West and South, for example, California and Florida both set a record high of 4,000+ cases in one day. However, in Illinois, it has been roughly stable with ~600 cases a day. 

I just finished working on coronavirus situation report (can be found on the home page). It discusses the latest numbers globally, and the current treatments that have been used in hospitals. 

Whats Happening Now:​

I am still studying for the GRE. I have been focusing on learning new vocabulary, along with doing practice questions. I am going to add on to the current situation report by using using biostatistic methods, such as calculating incident rates, and then adding a section on a comparison with the SARS outbreak in 2002. 

I am still reading the CDC's published textbook on the Principles of Epidemiology so I can get a better understanding of the concepts and learn new methods of comparison. ​

My first blog. 

By Sannidhi SarvadhavabhatlaJune 8, 2020

What Happened:

​Since quarantine has started in mid-March, I have been residing at my home in Illinois.  Currently in Illinois, we have moved into Phase 3 two weeks ago. Now, gatherings of 10 or fewer are allowed with social distancing. State parks have opened along with some childcare services. Barbershops, all non-essential businesses, and some retail stores. 

There is a huge gap between Phase 3 and Phase 4. Moving into Phase 4 probably wont happen for some time. It requires that the positivity rate is no higher than 20%, no overall increase in hospital admissions for coronavirus, and availability of hospital beds and necessary equipment. ​

Whats Happening Now:​

I am currently studying for the GRE and am hoping to take the exam in 2-3 months. I have been using various online resources, focusing on learning new vocabulary. Also, I just started working on a Coronavirus and SARS situation report by using various biostatistic methods to analyze the rates and transmissions of the two outbreaks and compare them. However, before I get further into that, I am currently reading the CDC's published textbook on the Principles of Epidemiology so I can get a better understanding of the concepts and learn new methods of comparison. ​

I have attached the link of the textbook below.  ​

Principles of Epidemiology in Public Health Practice, Third Edition 
An Introduction to Applied Epidemiology and Biostatistics
