Current News

 Johnson & Johnson - Is a Potential Vaccine Likely This Year?

Johnson and Johnsons chief scientific officer says that they are increasing manufacturing in order to start having clinical trials from September for a potential vaccine. The company wants to administer 1 billion vaccines by next year. It is possible for the vaccine to be out this year if the FDA can approve it with limited testing. The CEO doesn't believe that the virus will pass without a presence of a vaccine.  

Kawasaki Disease in Children - Link to Coronavirus

There is evidence from various states in the United States, Italy, France, and many other European countries that Kawasaki disease may be linked to coronavirus. "Eight of the 10 children tested positive for coronavirus antibodies" an Italian research says. Researchers concluded that it is possible the inflammatory disease appears to be a response after the recovery from the virus.

Cats can Infect Other Cats

Latest research suggests that cats can infect other cats, but not show any symptoms. In a study with cats with the virus paired with the healthy cats, health cats contracted the virus, but only showing mild respiratory symptoms. They don't believe that cats are interfering with the spread of COVID-19 globally. However, researchers are studying whether there is a link in transmission from cats to humans.  

Coronavirus May be a Vascular Disease

In April, patients having blood clots appeared to be a new suprising symptom. Young people appeared to be dying from stroke related issues. When the virus enters the lungs it destroys the tissues and breaks open the blood vessels. The virus then can affect the linings of the blood vessel. The endothelial cells protect the vascular system and release proteins that can affect the immune system and also cause clotting.
